Question 3

Which of the following activation functions is most commonly used in hidden layers of deep neural networks due to its computational efficiency and ability to mitigate the vanishing gradient problem?

Explanation

ReLU (Rectified Linear Unit) is the most commonly used activation function in hidden layers of deep neural networks for several reasons:

  • It's computationally efficient (simply returns max(0, x))
  • It helps mitigate the vanishing gradient problem that affects sigmoid and tanh functions
  • It introduces non-linearity without affecting the positive inputs
  • It allows for sparse activation (many neurons output 0)

While sigmoid and tanh functions were popular in earlier neural networks, they suffer from saturation and vanishing gradient problems. Softmax is typically used in the output layer for multi-class classification problems, not in hidden layers.
